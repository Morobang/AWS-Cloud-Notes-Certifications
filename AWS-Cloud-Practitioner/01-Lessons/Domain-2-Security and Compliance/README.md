# Domain 2: Security and Compliance

## Why security matters in the cloud

When you move workloads to AWS, you gain flexibility and scale — but security doesn't become AWS's problem. You and AWS share responsibility for it. Understanding exactly where that line sits is the foundation of this entire domain.

Security in the cloud covers three connected concerns:

**Security** — Protecting your data, applications, and accounts from unauthorized access and attacks.

**Compliance** — Meeting regulatory and industry requirements (HIPAA, GDPR, PCI DSS, etc.) that apply to your business.

**Governance** — The policies, controls, and oversight processes that keep your cloud usage organized, auditable, and cost-managed.

## Key terms

**Authentication** — Proving who you are. Username + password is authentication.

**Authorization** — Determining what you're allowed to do after proving your identity. IAM policies control authorization.

**Encryption at rest** — Data is encrypted when stored (in S3, RDS, EBS, etc.).

**Encryption in transit** — Data is encrypted while traveling over a network (HTTPS, TLS).

**MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)** — Requiring more than just a password. Usually password + a code from your phone.

**Principle of least privilege** — Give users only the minimum permissions they need. Nothing more.

**IAM** — Identity and Access Management. The AWS service that controls who can do what in your account.

**Compliance** — Conforming to standards and regulations such as HIPAA (healthcare), PCI DSS (payments), or GDPR (European privacy law).
