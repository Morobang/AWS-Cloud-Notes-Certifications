Task 4.1

AWS Pricing Models

How AWS charges for services and the options available to reduce costs.

AWS Free Tier

12-Month Free

New account benefits

  • 750 hrs/month EC2 t2.micro or t3.micro
  • 5 GB S3 Standard storage
  • 750 hrs/month RDS db.t2.micro
  • 30 GB EBS storage
Always Free

No expiry date

  • Lambda: 1M requests/month
  • DynamoDB: 25 GB storage
  • CloudWatch: 10 custom metrics
  • SNS: 1M publishes
Free Trials

Short-term trials

  • SageMaker: 2 months
  • Redshift: 2 months
  • Starts from first service use
  • Service-specific limits apply

Exam tip: Know the three categories. "Always Free" services (Lambda, DynamoDB) never expire. "12-month" benefits (EC2 t2.micro, S3 5 GB) end one year after account creation. "Free Trials" start on first use.

Core Pricing Principles

▶

Pay-as-you-go

No upfront fees, no contracts, no termination charges. Pay for compute hours, GB stored, GB transferred, or requests — only what you consume.

📅

Pay less when you reserve

1-year or 3-year commitments give 30–70% discounts. Reserved Instances lock to a service. Savings Plans commit to $/hour of compute spend — more flexible.

📈

Pay less with volume

Tiered pricing automatically applies as usage grows. S3 cost per GB drops at 50 TB and 500 TB thresholds. Consolidated billing combines usage across accounts.

EC2 Pricing Model Comparison

Model Best for Discount vs On-Demand Commitment
On-Demand Variable workloads, new projects, short-term — None
Reserved Instances Steady-state workloads running 1–3 years Up to 72% 1 or 3 years
Savings Plans Flexible compute across EC2/Lambda/Fargate Up to 66% 1 or 3 years ($/hr)
Spot Instances Fault-tolerant, flexible, interruptible workloads Up to 90% None (can be reclaimed)
Dedicated Hosts Compliance, bring-your-own-license requirements Varies On-Demand or 1/3 year

Data Transfer Pricing

Transfer direction Cost
Inbound to AWS (from internet) Free
Between AWS services in the same region Free or very low
Between AWS regions Charged per GB
Outbound to the internet Charged per GB (volume discounts apply)
Task 4.2

Billing & Cost Management

Tools to monitor, analyze, estimate, and control AWS spending.

🔔

AWS Budgets

Set spend or usage limits and receive alerts before you exceed them. Supports actual and forecasted threshold alerts. Can trigger SNS actions.

  • Cost budget ($ amount)
  • Usage budget (hours, GB, etc.)
  • RI/Savings Plan coverage budget
  • RI/Savings Plan utilization budget
📊

AWS Cost Explorer

Visualize and analyze up to 12 months of historical cost data. Break down by service, region, account, or tag. Includes right-sizing recommendations for EC2.

  • Forecasts next 12 months
  • RI and Savings Plans reports
  • Cost anomaly detection
  • Right-sizing recommendations
🧮

AWS Pricing Calculator

Estimate costs for a proposed architecture before deploying. No AWS account required. Compare architectural options and regions.

  • 150+ AWS services
  • Compare regions side-by-side
  • Export as PDF or CSV
  • Access at calculator.aws
🏠

Consolidated Billing

AWS Organizations combines billing across all accounts into one invoice. Usage is pooled for volume discounts. RI benefits share across accounts.

  • Single bill, one payment method
  • Per-account cost breakdown
  • Aggregate usage for tier pricing
  • RI and Savings Plan sharing
🏷

Cost Allocation Tags

Key-value labels on resources that appear as columns in billing reports. Activate in the Billing console before they appear in reports.

  • AWS-generated (createdBy, stack-name)
  • User-defined (Department, Project, CostCenter)
  • Used for charge-backs and reporting
📄

Cost and Usage Report (CUR)

The most granular billing data — line-item cost per resource, delivered as CSV/Parquet to S3 daily or monthly. Feed into Athena, QuickSight, or BI tools.

  • Hourly or daily granularity
  • RI amortization included
  • Tag values per resource

Budgets vs Cost Explorer: Budgets is forward-looking — it alerts you before you overspend. Cost Explorer is backward-looking — it helps you understand what already happened. Both are used together but serve different purposes.

Need Service
Get alerted before exceeding a spend limit AWS Budgets (with forecasted alert)
Analyze which services cost the most last quarter AWS Cost Explorer
Estimate cost of a new architecture before building AWS Pricing Calculator
Combine billing across 20 AWS accounts into one invoice AWS Organizations consolidated billing
Track costs by department or project Cost allocation tags + Cost Explorer or CUR
Export detailed per-resource billing data for BI tools Cost and Usage Report (CUR)

The authoritative reference for every service. Includes user guides, API references, CLI guides, getting-started tutorials, and FAQs. Start here for any service question.

Searchable collection of answers to common questions and error troubleshooting. Covers specific error messages and step-by-step solutions across all services.

Community Q&A platform (successor to AWS Forums). AWS employees provide official answers. Use when you want community input or to see how others solved similar problems.

🔒

AWS Health Dashboard

🌐

Service Health Dashboard (Public)

Shows real-time status of all AWS services globally. When AWS has an outage or service disruption, this is where it is reported. Accessible at status.aws.amazon.com — no login required.

👤

Personal Health Dashboard (AWS Health)

Shows events and notifications that specifically affect your AWS account and resources. Includes scheduled maintenance, degradations impacting your instances, and required actions. Accessed via the AWS Console.

AWS Marketplace

Digital catalog of third-party software: pre-configured AMIs, SaaS subscriptions, data products. Purchases consolidated into your AWS bill. Enterprise customers can create a Private Marketplace.

Task 4.1 — Pricing Models

A startup is experimenting with a new application. The workload is unpredictable and the project may be cancelled within 3 months. Which EC2 pricing model minimizes financial risk?

A3-year Reserved Instances with all-upfront payment
BOn-Demand pricing
CCompute Savings Plans
DDedicated Hosts
Answer: B. On-Demand pricing has no upfront commitment and no cancellation cost. The startup pays only for hours used and can stop at any time. Reserved Instances and Savings Plans require 1–3 year commitments — if the project is cancelled, the commitment cost remains. Dedicated Hosts are for compliance or BYOL licensing requirements, not cost minimization for uncertain workloads.

Task 4.1 — Free Tier

A company's AWS Lambda function has been running for 18 months and processes 800,000 invocations per month. What does the AWS Free Tier provide for this usage?

ANothing — the 12-month free tier has expired
BThe first 750 hours of Lambda execution per month
CThe first 1 million requests per month at no charge
DAll 800,000 invocations — Lambda is always free
Answer: C. Lambda's Free Tier (1 million requests/month + 400,000 GB-seconds) is categorized as "Always Free" — it never expires and applies to all AWS customers regardless of account age. Since this account is 18 months old, the 12-month tier has ended, but the Always Free benefits continue. All 800,000 invocations fall within the 1 million threshold, so Lambda is free this month. The 750-hour tier applies to EC2, not Lambda.

Task 4.1 — Data Transfer

An application in us-east-1 reads data from an S3 bucket in the same region. Users in Europe then download that data from the application servers. Which data transfers incur charges?

ABoth: EC2 to S3 within the region AND outbound to Europe
BOnly EC2 to S3 within the region
COnly outbound data transfer to Europe (to the internet)
DNeither — all AWS data transfer is free
Answer: C. Data transfer between AWS services within the same region (EC2 reading from S3 in us-east-1) is free. Data transfer outbound from AWS to the internet — such as serving content to users in Europe — is charged per GB. Inbound transfer (from internet to AWS) is also free.

Task 4.2 — Budgets vs Cost Explorer

A finance team wants to receive an email notification if AWS spending is predicted to exceed $8,000 before the end of the current month. Which tool and configuration is correct?

AAWS Cost Explorer — set a monthly report at the $8,000 threshold
BAWS Budgets — create a cost budget with a forecasted alert at $8,000
CAWS Pricing Calculator — enter $8,000 as the maximum estimate
DAWS Cost and Usage Report — set an alert threshold in the S3 export
Answer: B. AWS Budgets supports forecasted alerts, which trigger when AWS projects that spending will exceed the threshold before the billing period ends — exactly the "predicted to exceed" requirement. Cost Explorer analyzes historical data and can detect anomalies, but does not set budgets with proactive notifications. Pricing Calculator estimates costs before deployment; it does not monitor live spending. CUR is a data export — it has no alerting capability.
5
Task 4.2 — Cost Tools

A solutions architect is designing a new multi-tier web application and needs to estimate the monthly AWS cost before getting budget approval. Which tool is most appropriate?

AAWS Cost Explorer
BAWS Budgets
CAWS Pricing Calculator
DAWS Cost and Usage Report
Answer: C. The AWS Pricing Calculator is the pre-deployment cost estimation tool. You configure the planned services (EC2 instance type, RDS, ALB, S3, etc.) and it produces a monthly cost estimate — before anything is deployed. No AWS account is required. Cost Explorer analyzes historical spending from deployed resources. Budgets sets limits on spending. CUR exports billing data from resources that are already running.

Task 4.3 — Support Plans

A company runs a mission-critical trading platform that requires a dedicated Technical Account Manager and a 15-minute response time for critical outages. Which AWS Support plan meets both requirements?

ABusiness Support
BEnterprise On-Ramp
CEnterprise Support
DDeveloper Support
Answer: C. Only Enterprise Support provides both a dedicated TAM (not a shared pool) and a 15-minute response time for business-critical outages. Enterprise On-Ramp provides access to a pool of shared TAMs and a 30-minute critical response — neither matches the requirements. Business Support has a 1-hour critical response and no TAM. Developer Support has no production-down support.

Task 4.3 — Trusted Advisor

A company on Developer Support wants to use Trusted Advisor to find all idle EC2 instances and unattached EBS volumes to reduce costs. What happens when they try?

AThey can access all Trusted Advisor checks — it is included on all plans
BThey cannot — cost optimization checks require Business Support or higher
CThey can access cost checks but not security checks on Developer Support
DTrusted Advisor is only available on Enterprise Support
Answer: B. Basic and Developer plans provide only 7 core Trusted Advisor checks, which focus on security (MFA on root, unrestricted security groups, public S3 buckets, EBS/RDS public snapshots) and service limits. Cost optimization checks — including idle EC2 instances and unattached EBS volumes — are only available on Business Support or higher, which provides access to the full set of 100+ checks across all five categories.

Task 4.3 — Health Dashboard

A company's operations team notices their application is slow. They want to check whether AWS is experiencing any service disruptions in their region AND whether any scheduled maintenance is planned for their specific EC2 instances. Which resources address each need?

ABoth are answered by the Service Health Dashboard
BBoth are answered by the Personal Health Dashboard
CService disruptions: Service Health Dashboard; Maintenance on their instances: Personal Health Dashboard
DService disruptions: Personal Health Dashboard; Maintenance on their instances: Service Health Dashboard
Answer: C. The Service Health Dashboard (status.aws.amazon.com) shows real-time status of AWS services globally — public, no login needed. It answers "is AWS having an outage?" The Personal Health Dashboard (AWS Health, in the Console) shows events that affect your specific account and resources, including scheduled maintenance for your EC2 instances and degradations impacting your workloads. Each dashboard serves a distinct purpose.
